Explaining and Mitigating Attribute Imbalance

Abstract

Neural networks often exhibit attribute imbalance, where they rely on simple attributes over other crucial ones. Recent studies focused on discovering novel imbalances in the form of bias-aligned and bias-conflicting groups. However, precisely identifying imbalanced attributes has received relatively limited attention. Especially in multi-channel source separation, existing methods are inapplicable, while the identification is important because manual inspection of every audio data is more time-consuming than in image. In this work, we propose a counterfactual explanation and imbalance mitigation method, named Counterfactual Attribute Suppression (CAS). CAS unambiguously identifies imbalanced attributes and effectively mitigates the identified imbalance. CAS is validated in image classification and sound source separation, with a particular emphasis on its efficacy in efficiently identifying imbalanced attributes and effectively leveraging worst-group performance in sound domain.

Effectivenss of Counterfactual Explanation

To demonstrate the effectiveness of our method (CAS), we compare two explanation methods: "Explanation Based on Random Samples" and "Counterfactual Explanation". The group labels are given as $D_{easy}$, $D_{hard}$ and $D_{easy+hard}$. In the explanation based on random samples, users identify the easy attribute causing imbalance by comparing samples between $D_{hard}$ and $D_{easy+hard}$. There are lots of attributes in samples, e.g, gender, timbre, spatial, speed and reverberation.

Therefore, it is challenging to identify which attribute contributes to imbalance by comparing $D_{hard}$ and $D_{easy+hard}$. However, in the counterfactual explanation, users can readily find out the easy attribute by comparing a sample with its counterfactuals, employing the attribute suppression model, $f_{suppression}$, to suppress the easy attribute of samples from $D_{easy}$.
